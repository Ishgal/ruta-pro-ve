## Design Context

### Users
University students, recent graduates, and bachilleres (high school grads) in Venezuela — age 18–28 — browsing on mobile or laptop. Primary context: sitting at home, motivated but frustrated that their degree alone isn't enough to find work. Core job to be done: find a clear, structured path from where they are to their first professional job in accounting (later, other careers).

### Brand Personality
Three words: **striving, grounded, precise**

Not aspirational in an airy way — *striving* because the audience is hungry, working against real economic adversity. *Grounded* because the platform speaks to the Venezuelan market specifically, not generic Latin America. *Precise* because the AI creates a tailored path, not a generic curriculum.

Emotional goal: the user should feel *seen* — like this platform was built for their exact situation — and *capable* — like success is within reach if they follow the path.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Ambitious and energetic. Bold, clear, high-contrast — not playful or childlike, not cold or corporate.
- **Theme**: Light overall (content-focused, accessible), with a dark navy hero (`#0D2040`) as an impactful entry point.
- **Reference feel**: Closer to a premium career-training platform than a children's education app. Think Mimo or Brilliant — clean and purposeful — but warmer and more Latin American in voice.
- **Anti-references**: Avoid anything that looks like a generic Udemy clone (gray backgrounds, blue pill buttons everywhere), and avoid the "duolingo green + owl" playful-childish treatment.
- **Colors**: Navy `#1B4F8C` as brand primary, teal `#00B5B5` as accent and active-state color, amber `#F59E0B` for gamification (XP, streaks), light background `#F4F7FB`.

### Typography
- **Display/headings**: Bricolage Grotesque (variable, weights 400–800) — editorial grotesque with optical character. Bold headlines at 800 weight with fluid sizing (clamp).
- **Body**: Nunito (weights 400–700) — warm, rounded, accessible. Chosen for legibility and warmth in an educational context without being juvenile.
- **CSS variables**: `--font-bricolage` (display), `--font-nunito` (body)
- **Type scale**: Fluid headings (clamp), fixed rem scale for UI components.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness** — every section answers one question directly. No decorative copy.
2. **Show the product** — the hero uses a realistic UI mockup of the "Ruta de Aprendizaje" card to tell the story better than words.
3. **Gamification as identity** — XP, levels, and progress bars appear in the hero and course cards, making gamification visible from the first second.
4. **Venezuelan specificity** — course titles, testimonial names and institutions, and copy reference Venezuela explicitly. No generic Latin American content.
5. **High contrast, low noise** — few accent colors (teal and amber), most space is white or light blue-gray, making the accents feel intentional.

### Sections (Landing Page)

4. Featured courses — 3 cards with XP and gamification indicators

6. Final CTA — dark navy bookend section
7. Footer — minimal
